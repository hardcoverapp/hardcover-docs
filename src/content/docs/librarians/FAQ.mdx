*This page answers common questions about librarian permissions, workflows, and known platform quirks that can affect editing.*

## Known issues
*Before looking at the FAQ or guidelines, please note these following known issues:*

- If a book belongs to a series but does not appear on the series page, check the `Place in series (text)` field. If this field containts anything other than a number, remove the extra text (for example, "Vol"., "tome", or short story titles), then save the book. The book should then appear on the series page.
- Series merges currently do not remove and redirect the de-duplicated series. This can cause the de-duplicated series to be recreated by later book imports.

## Frequently Asked Questions

*The Librarian docs are fairly comprehensive, but a few questions are asked often enough to address here.*

### What prevents a librarian from making harmful edits to a book/author/etc?
Books and editions with an `impact_score` of 5 or higher (meaning anything with five or more reads) are automatically queued for additional approval which requires a member of the Hardcover Senior Librarian team to check and confirm the edits before any changes take place.

### How do I report bugs?
Hardcover is under active development by a small team, so issues may appear as features are added or updated. The best place to report a bug is via <a href={URLS.BUGS_DISCORD} target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Discord</a> in the #bugs channel. 

Before posting, do a quick search to check whether the issue has already been posted. If not, provide details relating the the bug such as: 
- A link to the page you were editing;
- The action you took;
- What happened as a result;
- Any error messages or unexpected behavior
- Screenshots if relevant;
- Console output if you’re comfortable with browser dev tools.

Reporting bugs in Discord allows other <a href={URLS.LIBRARIAN_DISCORD} target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">#librarians</a> or developers to confirm whether an issue is known, suggest a work around, or escalate it for review. We truly appreciate bug reports as they help us improve Hardcover for everyone, and we appreciate the time taken to report them.

Thank you for your reports and your patience!
